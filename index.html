Online Ticketing

Customers select an attraction, pick a date/time, and pay via Stripe. They get a QR code ticket emailed instantly. Staff scan at the gate.

Merch Store

8 products live (tees, hoodies, hats, beanies). Size selection, Stripe checkout, shipping address collection. Inventory tracked per size.

Admin Dashboard

Create ticket windows, manage capacity, view all orders, track revenue, update inventory, manage food truck schedules — all from one place.

QR Code Scanner

Browser-based scanner for gate staff. Scan a ticket QR code, see if it's valid, mark it as used. Works on any phone.

Mailing List + SMS

Email and SMS opt-in with TCPA-compliant consent. Build an audience you own instead of renting reach from Facebook.

Branded Email Confirmations

HTML emails for ticket and order confirmations — with QR codes, order details, and farm branding. Professional from first touch.

Food Truck Scheduling

Admin interface to set food truck vendors and dates. Public page shows the upcoming schedule automatically.

Professional Branding

Custom design system with Green Acres colors, typography, and visual language. Consistent across site, emails, and tickets.

Brand Value

Why Branding Matters More Than You Think

$

Price Perception

A professional brand lets you charge what the experience is worth. Visitors expect to pay more when the packaging matches the product. $15 feels like a deal when the brand looks premium.

@

Own Your Audience

Facebook reach has declined 60% since 2018. An email list and SMS subscribers are yours forever. No algorithm changes, no pay-to-play. One email blast to 5,000 subscribers is worth more than a post seen by 2,000.

T

Walking Billboards

Every t-shirt, hoodie, and hat sold is free advertising. Merch turns customers into brand ambassadors. A $25 tee markets the farm every time it's worn — at school, at church, at the grocery store.

The Email List

The Most Valuable Asset You Don't Have Yet

Every ticket buyer and merch customer automatically joins the mailing list. Within one season, you'll have thousands of subscribers you can message for free, anytime.

  • 1
    Tulip season opens March 15 One email blast to 5,000 subscribers. 10% buy tickets online. That's 500 tickets × $10 = $5,000 before you even open the gate.
  • 2
    New hoodie drops in October Email your list. 3% convert at $48 avg. 150 orders = $7,200 in a single email send. Zero ad spend.
  • 3
    Pumpkinville tickets going fast Scarcity email: "Only 200 spots left this Saturday." Creates urgency, drives pre-sales, helps you plan staffing.
  • 4
    Holiday gift guide in December Merch makes great gifts. A curated email in early December drives off-season revenue when the farm is closed.
